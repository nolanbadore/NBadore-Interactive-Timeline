the history, present, and future of

ESPORTS

In the past 6 decades esports has grown from a bunch of nerds competing for a subscription to the Rolling Stones magazine to professionally trained athletes competing with millions of dollars on the line.

November 1, 1980

THE FIRST

The National Space Invaders Tournament. On this day the first official gaming tournament was held. The gamers competed in front of a whopping 10,000 spectators.

March 8th, 1990

NINTENDO

The Nintendo World Championship began on March 8th, 1990 in Dallas, Texas. It ended up touring through 29 cities across the United States. The top two scorers then competed for the title of City Champion. The finalists won a trophy, $250, and a trip for two to the World Finals at Universal Studios Hollywood in Los Angeles, California.

March 31st, 1998

STARCRAFT

Starcraft is one of the largest esports of all time. In 2002, pro-gamers started to become organized into teams that were sponsored by large South Korean companies like Samsung, SK Telecom, and KT. Over $4,000,000 in prize money has been awarded in total.

January 1st, 2000

KESPA

The Korea e-Sports Association is a South Korean group that manages esports for many different games within South Korea. Notable esports include StarCraft: Brood War and, since May 2012, StarCraft II.

January 1st, 2002

MLG

Major League Gaming held gaming tournaments throughout North America in the early 2000's and 2010's. MLG was founded with the goal of elevating competitive video games to a viable spectator sport.

2005

CPL

The 2005 CPL World Tour was a year long gaming competition held by the Cyberathlete Pro League. The CPL World Tour made 9 stops worldwide and then the final event was held in NYC. The total prize pool for the event was $1,000,000.

2010

SCANDAL

In 2010 Korean pro players illegally fixed and bet on professional StarCraft matches. The pro players were approached by online betting websites and agreed to throw games for financial gain. These activities are strictly illegal under Korean law and many of the players were banned from esports and some faced jail time.

2011

TWITCH

Twitch is a live streaming platform focused towards gaming. Hundreds of gaming competitions are streamed to millions of viewers with Twitch being the main avenue for these competitions to be shown to the public.

July 2013

PROFESSION

In July of 2013, the US Government officially recognized League of Legends professional players as pro athletes. This status would allow them to recieve work visas to visit the US for tournaments.

2016-2019

OVERWATCH

The Overwatch League was formed. The league features 20 teams franchised by the likes of the Kraft groub, Hersh Interactive Group, Cloud9, and many more. In the 2020 season the League will feature home and away games throughout an entire season much like other professional sports.

2019

FORTNITE

The 2019 Fortnite World Cup. One of the largest prize pools in all of esports. Pro's from all around the world attended this event in order to prove their skill. The event took place in NYC and the prize pool was an incredible $30 million. The winner of the Solo tournament was 'Bugha' a 16 year old pro. He walked away with $3 million

2019

DOTA

The International Nine. The DOTA 2 tournament smashed the record for the highest prize pool in an esports tournament. The total prize pool was $34,330,068.
